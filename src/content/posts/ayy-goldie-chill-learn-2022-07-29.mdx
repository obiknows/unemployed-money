### some back story

I started out in the stock market doin 0DTE SPX credit spreads the (bread 🍞 n butta' 🧈 strategy), 3x a week to pull some income up out the markets.

Since then, I had learned alot from blogs, Youtube, reading, and experience that I added on to my strategy.

which turned into:

- sell credit spreads on the SPX500 (bread 🍞 n butta' 🧈)
- go long/short with the SPX500 using futures contracts [/MES, /ES]
- buy naked options on some stocks i mess with [TSLA, AMD, COIN, SQ]

and do random plays based on news like:

- oil futures
- bond futures (/ZB zebra bonds 🦓 ayy 🤟🏿)
- earnings option plays
- currency futures plays (/6E 🇪🇺 euro fade on Russia war stories)

all of this has been a learning experience.

i figured out, that i could be making way more money doing a variety of plays, as opposed to just my one credit spread 3x a week.

so my upside is great b/c I have more exposure, so the opportunity to make more is increased (more reward).

but at the same time, i'm putting more money on the line (more risk)

and heres the key 🔑

because 90% of my plays are in the stock market, they're correlated (move together/hand in hand) whether its futures or stock options (and even BONDS...)

meaning that... theres a lot of risk that I'm exposed too and its compounded b/c I'm basically trading multiple variations of the same thing.

### tl;dr

**TOO MUCH RISK**. gotta find ways to take some risk off the table, while maximizing reward

getting back to the (bread 🍞 n butta' 🧈 strategy™️)

analyzing the portfolio way deeper

improving education.

### 3 minutes to market open

I'm trying something new here. I saw that from the PoC ([point of control](https://www.google.com/search?q=point+of+control+volume+profile)) from the last session to this session, there was *a zone* that seemed to appear.

My thinking was that, although price is dropping right now, this zone has 2 areas of high volume, meaning alot of orders occur at these levels so price *may* revisit here again.

![](/images/07-29/07-29_4.27.24.png)

But, I had a bullish assumption overall because from last session till now, the point of control *increased*. That means that theres even more demand, so I'd have to classify this zone as a [demand zone](https://www.google.com/search?q=demand+zone) (a.k.a support, place where buying happens, etc)

So going into open, I think theres going to be some buying. So I'll hold through the -$200 drawdown

### 15 minutes into the open

###### SPX Credit 💳 Spread (Bull 🐂 Put Spread)

I took this position b/c I'm bullish on the market for the week. Especially after the big dawgs reported Earnings (AMZN, AAPL, GOOGL) and were all bullish

Market initially pushed down and I used that opportunity of that counter movement to enter PUT spread at 4090/4085 [4090/4085 means I sold -1 4090 PUT and bought +1 4085 PUT to limit my risk, a.k.a. covering, financing, hedging...etc]

This was followed by a strong bullish push which carried the position to some quick profits.

The max i drew down was around -$100-150 which is way better on the trade psychology (your mental game 🧠🧐) considering i was using *$2,500 of buying power* to put on the trade. This is one reason why I like credit 💳spreads as opposed to single (naked 😈) options or futures 🔮 contracts.

***ALMOST FORGOT.***

I stayed in the trade because I was also watching the [OBV](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/active-trading/041814/four-most-commonlyused-indicators-trend-trading.asp#toc-on-balance-volume-obv) (on-balance volume). I like OBV because it helps me visualize the flow of money in and out of the market. Its a tool I use to see what the big money is doing and if that goes in hand with what im doing right now.

When the market pushed down, the OBV was showing higher

![](/images/07-29/07-29_6.06.44.png)
