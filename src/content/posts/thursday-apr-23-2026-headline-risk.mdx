### WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED

the Iran headlines were real headline risk.
that part i couldn't control.

but everything else? that was on me.

i didn't trim any contracts when i was
in profit early. got a little greedy
waiting for more.

<Giphy url="https://giphy.com/embed/PyoyQRPyZXYq7mfxxs" />

then as theta decayed through the session,
my positions got weaker. less buffer.
more susceptible to exactly the kind of
shock that hit mid-day.

by the time the news dropped i had
no cushion. just exposure.

on top of that — held past admin stops
on TSLA, went further OTM on AAPL to
save premium (backwards logic), and
kept going back to names that were
already showing me they weren't working.

**the news was the trigger.**
**the setup was the gun.**

## POST GAME 💰⛹🏿‍♂️

##### SO WHAT DID WE LEARN

* **TRIM INTO PROFIT. ALWAYS.** when contracts
  are working early, take some off. you can't
  get hurt by locking in gains. greed is how
  theta and headlines cook you simultaneously.
* **HONOR THE STOP ON SIGNALS.** if the admin
  who called the trade got out, you get out.
  full stop. holding past someone else's stop
  on someone else's signal is not a strategy.
* **FURTHER OTM TO SAVE MONEY IS BACKWARDS.**
  going to 282.5 instead of 275 to cut premium
  cost me more in loss percentage. cheaper
  entry is not better entry when you're already
  fighting the tape.
* **OVERLEVERAGE NEAR ATH'S IS ASKING FOR IT.**
  big directional call positions near all time
  highs with no hedge. one bad headline and
  there's nowhere to hide.
* **SOXS IS THE LONGER PLAY.** not judging
  that one by today. 1-3 week thesis. watching.
