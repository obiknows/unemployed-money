### 5:40 PM (+3)

Aight so day started cool in the morning.

Was prolly a lil impatient in entering, but I went short in the /MES 3 contracts with a bear credit spread at 3940/3940 in the /ES futures options.

I also paired this with a short contact in the /ZB (US 30 Year Treasury Bonds) because theres an FOMC meeting where the fed supposed to raised interest rates and with that, we usually supposed to see bond yields rise and bond prices to fall.

the thing is tho, timing entry of all this proved to be real hard for me.

i saw the signs to short but looking back on it, i think i jumped the gun and should have waited to place entries **closer to the news event.** That way I'm not exposed to risk and uncertainty unnecessarily.

---

### 7:50 PM (+3)

Aight so still experiencing some strong drawdown.

Account is currently **-16.3%** into the meeting.

### 10:25 PM (+3)

Held trades in FOMC meeting. They *raised the interest rate by 75 bps* and the market **did not react**, in fact, it shot up the other way.

My credit spread isn't doing to bad, but the 3 /MES short contracts are down bad, *around $1,500*. Looks like the market don't give AF about this FOMC meeting. VIX is low low, like around 23 for what is usually a very volatile event.

In better news though, the bond futures did what i said, post rate hike, they retraced all the way to flat on the session, which is looking like a big enough candle if it holds to show a higher timeframe reversal.

Account is currently **-28.64%** into the meeting.

TBH, this is the worst I've been trading.

I had just got the account up to around $20k from $13k for the second time in 2 weeks, and over-leveraged on positions near the end of the week and ended up giving back **ALL of the gains, plus more**. I am clearly not doing a good enough job of cutting losers and letting winners run
