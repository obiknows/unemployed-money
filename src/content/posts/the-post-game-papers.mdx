## A collection of insights from Post-Game Reports after each my trade days.

---

### Week of 08/27/2022:

1. That you should buy more time, or if you're going 0DTE, reduce your risk (no more than $500 risk for now)
	- TRY SELLING LONGER DATED OPTIONS (7+ DTE) FOR LESS STRESS AND CONSISTENT PROFITS

2. Longer dated, neutral plays with undefined risk aren't my thing (for now)
3. INDICATORS ARE CONFUSING AF and don't help if your trigger happy like me 🔫
	- TRY NOT USING INDICATORS. FORWARD TEST (DEMO), AND SEE WHATS GOODIE

### 09/19/2022

- **Set It and Forget It (WALK AWAY)**
	- it makes more sense now to set the trade up, set the SL (stop loss), TP (take profit), and walk away. Tightening stops changes the POP (probability of profit) by reducing the area that price can be in without stopping out the trade. It's even worse when you move stops into profit ***before*** the trade hits TP. I know moving stops to breakeven still makes sense, but trailing the stops today ***cost me money***, even though the whole ideology behind moving the stops is in fact to SAVE MONEY.

- **1-3 TRADES MAX (DON'T OVERTRADE)**
	- I have a tattoo that reminds of this rule but the gist is that you can't really make more than 1-2 large emotional decisions in a day, so in order to trade more *efficiently* and prevent overtrading, it makes sense to have an absolute limit on trades. 1-3 max per account / day. Or maybe just in general. Idk. but for now, just 1-3 on the TopStep per day. can't be out here overtrading.

### 09/21/2022

- **DON'T ADD TO POSITIONS IN TOPSTEP, yet**
	not yet. you haven't really mastered that yet bro, so you just gotta chill and be patient with your SET AND FORGET. another example where you would've have $200+. so now you gotta hit a few 50 clips (pushups, sit-ups, squats).

- **NEVER RE-ENTER**
	once you're out you're out. new rule. you gotta look for a new entry on a HIGHER TIMEFRAME or a new position but you can't retry to do the same thing. doing the same thing and expecting a different result is INSANITY, especially if its gonna fuck up your bread.

- **ITS BETTER TO BE LATE THAN WRONG**
	pretty much self-explanatory. it pays to be patient. the question is can you remember that in the moment, and will you wait and react, rather than predicting?
