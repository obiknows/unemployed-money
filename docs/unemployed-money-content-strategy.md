# unemployed.money — Content & Video Strategy
> *Last updated: April 2026*

---

## The Content Ratio

**80% Trading** — journals, analysis, positions, market reads, lessons. The main character. What people follow you for.

**20% AI/Infra** — occasional behind the scenes. How the machine works. Infrequent but consistent.

Not two blogs. One publication with an occasional side door that says *"want to see how this gets made?"*

---

## The Two Trade Types — How They Coexist

### Short Term — The Heartbeat
Active, fast, thesis driven, time sensitive. The daily/weekly content. Live thought process, real entries, POST GAME every time.

> *"Entered 2 SPY puts at open. Fed speak at 2pm. Here's why."*

### Long Term — The Skeleton
Slow, conviction based, fundamental. The portfolio layer running underneath all the active trading. Shows you're not just chasing moves — you have a real thesis underneath.

> *"Here's what I'm holding long. Here's why I'm still in it. Here's what would change my mind."*

**Together they show a complete trader.** Short term is the activity. Long term is the foundation. Both in public builds credibility that neither does alone.

## Content Formats

### 1. Trade Journal (Daily / Per Session)
Short term trades. Fast, reactive, in the moment. The core of the blog.

**Structure:**
```
[MARKET OVERVIEW — Koyfin or TradingView screenshot]

HH:MM AM/PM (+timezone)
Entries, thesis, what you're seeing in real time

[Chart screenshots inline]

---

POST GAME 💰⛹🏿‍♂️
SO WHAT DID WE LEARN
• bullet lessons, no fluff

[Tags: tickers, strategy, macro event]

🎲
```

---

### 2. Analysis Post (Weekly)
Deeper dives. Earnings plays, macro reads, sector breakdowns, options strategy breakdowns. Authority-building content. Searchable. Evergreen.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — bold claim or question]

[The setup — what's happening fundamentally + technically]

[Your thesis — why you think what you think]

[The trade — structure, entries, reasoning]

[Tags]

🎲
```

---

### 3. Monthly Portfolio Update
Long term positions only. Calm energy, different tone from the journals. One post, one short video segment.

**What to cover:**
- What changed in the long book this month
- What you're still holding and why
- What would change your mind on any position
- Any new conviction adds

---

### 4. The Crossover Post (When It Happens)
Your most sophisticated content. When your long term thesis directly affects a short term trade.

> *"I'm long financials long term but I shorted XLF today because of this specific setup. Here's how those two things coexist."*

This shows multi-timeframe thinking. Most retail traders don't operate this way. You do. Show it.

---

### 5. AI / Infra Post or Video (Quarterly)
Behind the scenes. How the machine works. Not a tutorial — a transparent look at how you operate as a solo trading publication.

Examples:
- "Here's how I go from raw trade notes to a published post using AI agents"
- "My full Paperclip setup for running unemployed.money"
- "The prompt that writes in my voice"

## Video Strategy

### The Setup
- **Windows Desktop + 2 Monitors** — trading content
- **MacBook** — coding and AI infra content
- **DSLR** — face cam, always
- **OBS** — handles everything

### OBS Scenes (Keep It Simple)

**Scene 1: Chart + Face**
Monitor 1 (TOS / TradingView) as main capture. DSLR as picture-in-picture bottom right. Default look for all trading content.

**Scene 2: Full Face**
DSLR only. Intros, outros, talking head moments.

---

### The Recurring Video Formats

#### Weekly Market Recap (The Anchor) — Every Week
Your one non-negotiable. Same format, same energy, every week.

- What happened in the markets this week
- Your active trades — what you did, what worked, what didn't
- What you're watching going into next week
- Your positioning and thesis
- **Last 2-3 minutes:** quick long term portfolio update

**Length:** 5-10 minutes. Tight. No fluff.
**Machine:** Windows desktop. Charts on monitor 1. DSLR face cam.

---

#### Trade Breakdown (As They Happen)
Not on a schedule — triggered by interesting trades. Wins and losses. Losses are better content.

- Here's what I saw
- Here's why I entered
- Here's what happened
- Here's what I learned

**Length:** 3-7 minutes. Raw, not polished.
**Machine:** Windows desktop. Screen record TOS/TradingView, voice over.

---

#### AI / Infra Deep Dive (Quarterly)
One real behind the scenes video per quarter. Shows the full pipeline. This is the content that travels outside your core trading audience into tech Twitter.

**Length:** 10-20 minutes. More produced than the others.
**Machine:** MacBook screen share + DSLR face cam.

---

### Video Frequency — Realistic Commitment

| Format | Frequency | Non-Negotiable? |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly recap | Every week | Yes. Miss this and the habit breaks. |
| Trade breakdown | When a trade warrants it | No fixed schedule |
| AI/infra deep dive | Quarterly | Low pressure |

## The Portfolio Dashboard

Build a simple public-facing section on the blog. Updated manually or via agent. Gives readers the full picture at a glance.

```
LONG TERM POSITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────
Ticker | Entry | Current | % Return | Thesis | Since

RECENT SHORT TERM TRADES
────────────────────────────────────────────
Ticker | Type | Entry | Exit | P&L | Result
```

Not real time. Not automated. Just honest and current. Updated with the monthly portfolio post.

## The Full Weekly Machine

```
You trade → raw notes
        ↓
Agent turns notes into blog post (trade journal)
        ↓
Weekly recap video covers the same week
        ↓
X thread clips the key insight from both
        ↓
YouTube has the full video
        ↓
Blog has the full written version
        ↓
Monthly: long term portfolio update added to both
        ↓
Quarterly: AI/infra video shows how all of this gets made
```

One week of trading becomes a blog post, a video, and several X posts. All from the same raw material.
