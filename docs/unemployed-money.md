## Voice & Style Guide

### Who's Writing This
Ivy League educated, SWE by trade, day trader by passion. Speaks with full range — can explain theta decay and correlation risk in one sentence, drop "prolly not what you wanna do" in the next. The intelligence is never in question. The code-switching is intentional. This isn't dumbing down. This is fluency.

### The Five Voice Pillars

**1. Intelligent but never stiff**
Explain complex concepts naturally, inline, without breaking flow. Don't write like a textbook and don't write like a hype account. Write like someone who actually knows what they're talking about and doesn't need to prove it.

> *"I also paired this with a short contract in the /ZB (US 30 Year Treasury Bonds) because theres an FOMC meeting where the fed supposed to raised interest rates and with that, we usually supposed to see bond yields rise and bond prices to fall."*

Technical. Accurate. Unforced.

**2. AAVE and slang are features, not bugs**
Native vocabulary: `prolly`, `tryna`, `b/c`, `tho`, `AF`, `hella`, `ayy`, `crackin`, `lit`, `what's goodie`, `bandz`, `dang`. Never forced. Never overdone. Drops naturally in casual moments, disappears when you're being analytical.

**3. Radical honesty about numbers**
Don't soften losses. Ever. Show the exact number.
> *"Account is currently -28.64% into the meeting."*
That's the brand. That's what makes it real.

**4. ALL CAPS is a tool, not a habit**
Used for section emphasis (`POST GAME 💰⛹🏿‍♂️`), takeaways that matter (`FOLLOW YOUR RULES`), moments of genuine shock or conviction (`DOWN BAD`, `TOO MUCH RISK`). Not every other sentence. Earned, not sprinkled.

**5. We, not I**
The reader is on the journey with you.
> *"We still got fight in us"*
> *"so what did we actually learn?"*

### Brand Phrases — Own Them
- `bread 🍞 n butta' 🧈 strategy™️`
- `POST GAME 💰⛹🏿‍♂️`
- `🎲` — closing signature, every post
- `🦓 zebra bonds` — /ZB futures
- Parenthetical asides for inner monologue: `(your mental game 🧠🧐)`
- When something becomes a bit, formalize it with ™️

## GIF Placement Guide

GIFs are emotional punctuation. They mark a vibe shift. You don't explain them, you don't caption them. They just land.

| Moment | Vibe |
|---|---|
| Taking a big L | Shame/embarrassment |
| Rejecting a bad idea | Hard no — KG, Faze Rug |
| Celebrating a win | Hype — Ice Cube good day |
| Market does something wild | Reaction shot |
| Opening with a mantra | Energy setter |
| Bold call | Confidence |

**In raw notes, use placeholders:**
`%%gif: shame, took the L%%` or `%%gif: KG anything is possible%%`

Agent or you finds the right one at publish time. Don't let GIF hunting block the writing.

## SEO Without Killing The Voice

Your natural titles are unsearchable. The fix isn't killing the title — it's letting the slug and meta do the SEO work.

```
Title (your voice):   I Gotta Say, Today Was A Good Day 🥶
Slug (SEO):           /spy-puts-fomc-day-analysis-sept-2022
Meta description:     Broke down my SPY put thesis going into FOMC, 
                      caught the move, here's exactly what happened.
```

Title stays yours. Slug and meta work for Google and LLMs.

**For LLM visibility** — add an `llms.txt` file to the repo root. Emerging standard, basically a sitemap for AI crawlers. Clear, factual, structured writing also surfaces better in Perplexity, ChatGPT etc.
